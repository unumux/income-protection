Frequently Asked Questions:

  • How do I purchase disability insurance from Unum?

    Unum coverage is only available as an employee offering. Check with your employer's benefits representative to see if you're eligible or attend your company's next open enrollment session.

    For a list of commonly asked questions from employees, please visit http://www.unum.com/Employees/FAQs

  • Won't Workers' Compensation cover me if I'm unable to work?

    Most injuries are not work-related and therefore not covered by workers' compensation.4In fact, more than 3x as many injuries that require medical attention occur off the job than at work.5

  • What about Social Security Disability payments?

    Social Security Disabiity Insurance pays only for total disability that is expected to last for at least one year or result in death.6 Nearly 70% of workers applying for Social Security are denied. 7

  • How does disability insurance protect my income?

    Disability Insurance replaces a portion of your paycheck for a specific period of time (i.e. several weeks) if you can't perform your occupation due to injury or illness.

  • What does Unum disability insurance cover?

    Short term disability benefits can pay a portion of your income if you can't work for several weeks due to a covered injury or illness. Long term disability benefits replace a portion of your income if you become disabled for more than several weeks.

    For more detailed information on Unum disability insurance, please visit our Unum.com FAQ.

    http://www.unum.com/Employees/FAQs/Disability/FAQ

  • How much does disability insurance cost?

    The average monthly cost for long-term disability coverage is $20-$30.8

  • How do people typically get disability insurance?

    At least 90% of long term disability insurance owners obtained their coverage through the workplace.9
